Legal Industry News

RBG, R.I.P | A Few Personal Reminiscences on the Death of Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg from Professor David Post

A two-time law clerk for the late Justice Ginsburg reflects on her life and legacy, including her writing and editing standards, integrity, and the legal case she most enjoyed working on.

Ruth Bader Ginsburg, Supreme Court’s Feminist Icon, Is Dead at 87

Justice Ginsburg, who was known for her pioneering role in advancing gender-based equity, died of complications related to cancer after serving for 27 years on the nation’s highest court.

Why Did the Average Scaled Score for the July Multistate Bar Exam Improve?

Potential factors contributing to the increase include a higher percentage of test-takers finishing bar prep courses, and a greater proportion of first-time test-takers compared to 2019.

US Court Records Would Be Free Under House Judiciary Bill

Bipartisan legislation recently approved by the House Judiciary Committee would grant members of the public free access to federal court records housed in the PACER system.

Fake Websites for 4 BigLaw Firms Might Have Been Created to Get Deal Information

Newly declassified FBI documents show that the firms may have been targeted in an effort to obtain insider information related to mergers and acquisitions that were pending at the time.

Pay Gap Has Widened for Male and Female Partners in Larger Law Firms, New Report Says

Survey findings demonstrate that part of the reason for the disparity is that pay for male partners is going up at a faster rate than it is for female partners at the nation’s largest firms.

Tech News

Averting a possible ban of TikTok downloads in the US, President Trump approved a tentative deal giving Oracle and Walmart a minority stake in a US-based company that will run TikTok.

A federal judge in California has issued a preliminary injunction on free speech grounds blocking President Trump’s executive order seeking to ban Chinese app WeChat in the US.
