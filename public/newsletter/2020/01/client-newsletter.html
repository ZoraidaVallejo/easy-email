Tech News

Uber Agrees to Pay $4.4 Million to Settle EEOC Sexual Harassment and Retaliation Charge

Following a finding by the EEOC that Uber allowed a culture of sexual harassment and retaliation, the company will pay $4.4 million to victims and face three years of monitoring.

Trump Administration Says Employers Can Ban Organizing Via Company Email

The National Labor Relations Board has ruled that employees can be prohibited from using their work email for union organizing, overturning an Obama-era ruling stating the opposite.

Feds Reap Data From 1,500 Phones in Largest Reported Reverse-Location Warrant

In an effort to solve four Wisconsin arson cases, federal investigators secured search warrants allowing them access to location history data for about 1,500 smartphones.

Huawei Sues FCC to Stop Ban On Huawei Gear in US-Funded Networks

Seeking to overturn a decision that its equipment can’t be used in some government-funded telecom projects, Huawei has sued the FCC on due process and administrative law grounds.

SEO News

The Local Algorithm: Relevance, Proximity, and Prominence

This breakdown of Google's local pack outlines the three factors that have always driven the algorithm and therefore local rankings: relevance, proximity, and prominence.

Google Search Console Can More Accurately Report On Indexed Pages

An update to Google Search Console has resulted in some pages that were previously categorized as crawled but not indexed being indexed from December 15 onward.

Google: We Ignore Links From Sites Where Links Are Unlikely To Be Natural

Google’s John Mueller has stated that there is no need to disavow links from spammy domains, as Google already disregards them because they are not likely to be natural.

How Does Website Security Affect Your SEO?

It is more critical than ever for developers to shore up website security for SEO purposes by using an HTTPS domain distinction and preventing blacklisting through link spam.

Social Media News

New YouTube Policy Tries to Ban ‘Implied’ Threats, ‘Malicious’ Insults

After unsuccessful efforts at policing hateful content on its platform, YouTube plans to take a more aggressive stance against content creators instigating campaigns of harassment.

Social Media Platforms Leave 95% of Reported Fake Accounts Up, Study Finds

A new study has demonstrated not only the colossal scope of inauthentic social media activity, but also that most fake accounts remain active for weeks after being reported.

Instagram to Start Warning Users Before They Post ‘Potentially Offensive’ Captions

Utilizing AI-powered tools, Instagram will begin warning users if they are about to post a caption to a photo or video in their main feed if it’s similar to captions that have been reported.

Facebook Has 4 Out of 5 of the Most Downloaded Apps of the Year

The top 5 most downloaded apps worldwide of 2019 include Facebook Messenger, Facebook, WhatsApp Messenger, and Instagram, counting only new net downloads.

Legal Industry News

Percentage of Black Associates in Large Firms Finally Tops 2009 Level, But Not By Much

New data show increases in the numbers of law firm associates who are women or people of color compared to a decade ago, but the increase for black associates was troublingly small.

First-Year Class Sizes for ABA-Accredited Law Schools Are Slightly Smaller in 2019

While overall enrollment at ABA-accredited law schools is higher this year than last year, the number of first-year students is slightly lower, according to data from 203 institutions.

Law Firms Are ‘Poised to Grow’ As They Report More Success in Lateral Hiring

Driven primarily by an increase in billing rates, law firms are seeking to expand their equity partnerships through lateral hires, and also hire more junior and temporary lawyers.

Legal Jobs Take Another Leap and Hit 10-Year High; What’s Driving the Increase?

Data for November show an increase in hiring among law firms of all sizes, spurred largely by interest in recruiting related to capital markets, finance, and mergers and acquisitions.
