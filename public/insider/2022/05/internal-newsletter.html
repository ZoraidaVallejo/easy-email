UPCOMING HOLIDAYS

Dates to Remember
Mexico’s Labor Day
May 1st

During the Mexican Revolution back in 1913, the Casa del Obrero Mundial affiliates decided to commemorate May 1st as International Labor Day in Mexico publicly. Later in 1923, during the administration of President Alvaro Obregon, May 1st was declared Labor Day in Mexico.
Mother’s Day
US: May 8th MX: May 10th *

Mother's Day is an annual holiday that honors maternal work as the emotional support of families and society and recognizes women's essential role within the family. In 1914, Mother's Day was officially approved and declared a national holiday in the United States, while in Mexico, Mother's Day is celebrated on May 10th.
Memorial Day
May 30th

Memorial Day honors Americans who died serving their country in wars, and it is commemorated annually on the last Monday of May. In 1966 Congress declared Waterloo, New York, the holiday's birthplace. Today, it honors all those who have fallen in combat in the service of the United States.

*This is a non-federal holiday. It is not officially recognized. For more information about paid holidays please visit Justikia.
